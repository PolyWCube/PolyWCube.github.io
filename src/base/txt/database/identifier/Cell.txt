Definition: Cells are the basic structural and functional units of every higher organisation. They are the first organsation level to consider an organism as they contain all the characteristics of life.

Example: For instance, <a title = "Short for Escherichia Coli, is a gram-negative, facultative, anaerobic, rod-shaped and coliform bacterium that is commonly found in the lower intestine of warm-blooded organisms." href = "">E.coli</a> is a unicellular organism of the bacteria domain, can be used in medical production as it has the rapid cell cycle of 20 minutes.

Basic structure: All cells have some certain shared basic structure as every cell consists of cytoplasm containing genetic structure and organelle enclosed within a membrane. These are the most fundamental structural that create functional units suitable for life to exist and thrive.

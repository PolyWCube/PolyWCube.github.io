Organic

Organic compound is the chemical compound contain carbon, except for carbide, carbonate, carbon oxide and cyanide. The elements compose of the organical molecule must contain C (carbon), and usually contain H (hydrogen), O (hydrogen), N (nitrogen), halogen group elements include F (flourine), Cl (clorine),... , S (sulfur), P (phosphorus),... . They typically have low melting point, boiling point and tend to be hydrophobic.

The chemical reaction of organical matter tend to be slow, reaction in many direction and create a mixture of products.

Carbon Frame

At the atomic level, carbon is a non-metalic element with the atomic number of six. It's also a tetravalent, which mean a carbon atom can form up to 4 single covalent bond with other atoms or molecules. There are 3 isotypes occur naturally of carbon, the 12C and 13C are being stable, while the 14C is a radionuclide with the half-life of 5700 years.

The carbon frame refer to the carbon linkage together as the skeletal structure as it's the backbone of all organic matter. It's structure, the carbon frame formation and the covalent bond between the carbon molecules, creates some physical and chemical properties. Sometime, carbon frame refers as the C-base organic frame.

Hydrocarbon

The molecule contains only C-C and C-H bonds as it's only contains 2 element C (carbon) and H (hydrogen)

They naturally appear as fossil fuel (petroleum, natural gas and coal, or their hydrocarbon derivatives and purified forms).

Hydrocarbon derivative

More complex molecule with a hydrochain as the base, attached with other functional group

By changing the C-H bonds with other elements or functional groups which is called substitutability, it create the derivative of hydrocarbon, which is extremely diverse and complex in both terms of structure and properties. It's the reflection of carbon frame, include both physical and chemical properties.

Bond

Organic bonds mostly are covalent bond, follow with other less common bonds like hydrogen bond, sulfur-sulfur bond,...

Functional group

Functional group is the molecule or a group of molecule that can creates unique chemical properties.

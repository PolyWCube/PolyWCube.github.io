Organic

Organic compound is the chemical compound contain carbon, except for carbide, carbonate, carbon oxide and cyanide. They typically have low melting point, boiling point and tend to be hydrophobic.

Their bonds mostly are covalent bond, follow with other least common bond like hydrogen bond, sulfur-sulfur bond.

Carbon Frame

At the atomic level, Carbon is a non-metalic element with the atomic number of six. It's also a tetravalent, which mean a carbon atom can form up to 4 single covalent bond with other atoms or molecules. There are 3 isotypes occur naturally of Carbon, the 12C and 13C are being stable, while the 14C is a radionuclide, with the half-life of 5700 years.

The carbon frame refer to the carbon linkage together as the skeletal structure. It's the backbone of all organic matter. Sometime refer as the C-base organic frame.

Hydrocarbon

The molecule contains only C-C and C-H bonds as it's only contains 2 element C (carbon) and H (hydrogen)

By changing the C-H bonds with other elements or functional groups, it create the derivative of hydrocarbon, which is extremely diverse and complex in both terms of structure and properties.

Level Of Biological Organization

Levels of organization refer to the hierarchy of levels of organized physical matter that consider having characteristics of life, expanding from the microscopic scale of the atom, molecule, macromolecule, and cell to the global scale of the entire planet. Therefore, to emphasize and improve management, reductionism is an effective approach to dividing the complex system into simpler components.

The Hierarchy Of Organisation Level

Atomic level (Pre-cellular level)

  • Atom

    The basic particle of element or molecule.

  • Molecule

    The special sequence and structure of atom create organic matter in the first place for life to exist.

Cellular level

  • Organelle

    The group of molecule have a specify function and structure.

  • Cell

    The base of every higher level of organisation, create the structure and function of them.

    The first level to consider a organism.

Muti-cellular level

  • Tissue
  • Organ
  • Organ System
  • Body

    The physical form of muti-cellular organism, function and construct by mutiple organ system.

Ecological level

  • Group
  • Population
  • Ecosystem
  • Biosphere

    All life and environment that life can exist.

which can be divide into 2 catagories:

  • Fundamental level

  • Intermediate level

Emergent Property

Emergent properties are a set of phenomena that integrate and arise from the complex interactions and arrangements of components at the lower organisation level as a system. Each organisation level composed of the preceding level, which mean that the higher organisation level will inherited all the function and structure from the previous level. In addition to it, the difference organisation level compose of it interact together and form new property, which is the concept of emergence.

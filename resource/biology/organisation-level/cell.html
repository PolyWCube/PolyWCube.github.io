Basic Structure

All cells have some certain shared basic structure as every cell consists of cytoplasm containing genetic structure and organelle enclosed within a membrane. These are the most fundamental structural that create functional units suitable for life to exist and thrive.

Cell Membrane (Cytoplasmic Membrane)

The biological membrane separates and protects the interior of a cell from the extracellular environment composed of notably lipids and proteins, which include the two layers of phospholipid (lipid bilayer) with cholesterols interspersed between them, the glycoprotein, glycosaccharide and functional protein.

Cytoplasm (Cytosol)

The semifluid substance where all the vital processes occur and subcellular component suspended.

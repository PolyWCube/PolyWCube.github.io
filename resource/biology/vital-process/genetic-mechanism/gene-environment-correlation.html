Macromolecule

The large, complex monocules that are fundamental to the structure and function of organism. They are typically polymer compose of smaller, simpler units called monomer. There are 3 primary types of macromolecules:

  • Saccharide

    Also known as carbohydrate or glucide are the crucial micromolecule as it serves as the main energy source and structural component of organism.

  • Protein

    It is extremely dirverse, leading to a variety of function, include enzyme, structural component, transporter, antibodies, hormone,...

  • Nucleic Acid

    The essential component stores and transmits genetic code throught out sequence and structure for expression, adaption and evolution.
